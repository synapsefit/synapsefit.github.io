Why did my credit/debit card payment fail?
There are several reasons why your credit/debit card payment may have failed. Here are some common explanations:

Insufficient funds: The most common reason for payment failure is insufficient funds in your account. Make sure you have enough funds to cover the transaction amount.

Incorrect card details: Double-check the card number, expiration date, and CVV code to ensure they were entered correctly. Even a small error can result in payment failure.

Card expired: Check the expiration date on your card. If it has expired, you will need to use a different card or contact your bank to request a new one.

Security measures: Sometimes, banks may block or decline transactions for security reasons. This could be due to suspicious activity or if the transaction is outside your normal spending pattern. Contact your bank to confirm if there are any restrictions on your card.

Card restrictions: Card restrictions: Certain cards have usage restrictions imposed by the card issuer. For example, some cards can only be used for domestic transactions, while others may have limitations on online purchases. Verify with your bank if there are any restrictions on your card.

Technical issues: Occasionally, technical issues on the payment gateway or the merchant's side can lead to payment failures. Try again after a while or contact the merchant's customer support for assistance.

Blocked or frozen card: If you have previously reported your card as lost or stolen, it might be blocked or frozen. Similarly, if your bank suspects fraudulent activity on your card, they may temporarily block it. Contact your bank to resolve any card-related issues.

International transactions: If you are attempting an international transaction, ensure that your card is enabled for international payments. Some cards require prior authorization or activation to be used internationally.

If you continue to experience issues with your card payment, it's recommended to contact your bank or card issuer directly. They can provide specific information regarding the decline and offer assistance in resolving the problem.

How Long does it take for a refund?
The time it takes for a refund to be processed can vary depending on several factors. Here are some general guidelines:

Merchant processing time: Once you initiate a refund request, it typically needs some time to process it. This processing time can vary from a few business days to a couple of weeks. It's advisable to check the refund policy or contact our customer support to get an estimate of the processing time.

Payment method: The time it takes for the refunded amount to appear back in your account depends on the payment method used. Credit card refunds usually take 6-28 business days to reflect in your account, but it can take longer in some cases. Debit card refunds may take a similar amount of time, but it varies among banks. Refunds made through digital payment platforms like PayPal or mobile wallets can be almost instantaneous or take a few business days.

Bank processing time: Once we initiate the refund, your bank or financial institution needs to process it on their end. Banks may have different processing times, and it can take a few business days for the refund to be credited to your account.

Special circumstances: In some cases, such as during peak shopping seasons or if there are exceptional circumstances like product recalls or delays, the refund process may take longer than usual. We usually provide relevant information about such situations on our website or through customer communication channels.

It's important to note that these timeframes are general estimates and may vary depending on the specific circumstances. If you have concerns or questions about the status of your refund, it's recommended to reach out to our customer support or contact your bank for further assistance.

Delivery

What should I do if my order hasn't been delivered yet?
If your order hasn't been delivered within the expected timeframe, here are some steps you can take to address the issue:

Check the tracking information: If the merchant provided you with a tracking number, use it to track the status of your order. Visit the carrier's website or use their tracking system to see the latest updates on the shipment's whereabouts. Note any delays or delivery exceptions mentioned in the tracking information.

Contact us: Reach out to us and Provide us with your order details, including the order number and any relevant information. Inquire about the status of your order and express your concerns regarding the non-delivery. Our customer support should be able to assist you further and provide updates on the situation.

Review the delivery policy: Refer to the delivery policy to understand our estimated delivery times and any specific conditions or exceptions that may apply. Sometimes we may have longer processing or shipping times, especially during peak seasons or special promotions. Make sure you are within the expected delivery timeframe before taking further action.

Check for attempted delivery or delivery notices: If you were not available to receive the package, the carrier might have left an attempted delivery notice or taken the package to a local pickup location. Check your mailbox, doorstep, or any designated delivery areas for any missed delivery notices. If applicable, follow the instructions provided to arrange a redelivery or pickup.

File a complaint with the carrier: If the tracking information shows a significant delay or if there are issues with the carrier's service, you may need to file a complaint directly with the carrier. Contact their customer service and provide them with the tracking number and details of the non-delivery situation. They can investigate and provide guidance on the next steps.

Dispute the charge: If you have been charged for an undelivered item and all attempts to resolve the issue with us and carrier have been unsuccessful, you may consider disputing the charge with your payment provider (e.g., credit card company or PayHere). Provide them with all relevant documentation and details regarding the non-delivery to support your case.
